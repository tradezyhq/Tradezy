Fees & Pricing
How Tradezy makes money and what you'll pay
Q
How much does Tradezy charge?
▼
Tradezy charges a 5% platform fee to each party on every completed trade. There are no listing fees, no subscription fees, and no upfront costs. You only pay when a trade is successfully completed.
💡
Founding Traders — people who sign up before our July 17, 2026 launch — pay a lifetime rate of 3% instead of 5%. That discount is locked in forever.

Q
When exactly is the fee charged?
▼
The fee is collected via Stripe at the time an offer is accepted by both parties — before the meetup happens. This is how Tradezy can guarantee trade protection: if a dispute is filed and resolved in your favor, the fee can be refunded.
⚠️
If a trade falls through before the meetup, contact support at disputes@tradezyhq.com and we will review your case for a fee refund.
Q
Is it free to list an item?
▼
Yes — listing is completely free. You can post as many listings as you want with no cost. You only pay the 5% fee when a trade is completed. Browse, list, and make offers for free.

Item Value
How item values are set and what's fair
Q
Who decides what my item is worth?
▼
You do. Tradezy does not set item values — you enter your own estimated value when creating a listing. This works the same way as Facebook Marketplace or eBay — the owner knows their item best.
💡
We recommend checking recent sold listings on eBay, TCGPlayer (for cards), or Facebook Marketplace to get a realistic market value before listing. Accurate pricing gets you more and better offers.
Q
What if someone lists their item for way more than it's worth?
▼
Traders are free to list at whatever price they choose — but the market self-corrects. Overpriced listings get fewer offers and take longer to trade. Most traders quickly learn that accurate pricing leads to faster, better trades.

If you believe a listing is deliberately misrepresenting an item's value as part of a scam, use the Report button on the listing and our team will review it within 24 hours.
Q
What if the item isn't as described when I meet up?
▼
You are never required to complete a trade if the item is not as described. Before confirming the meetup in the app, inspect the item thoroughly. If it's not what was advertised:
1
Don't confirm the meetup in the app — only confirm after you're satisfied with the item.
2
File a dispute through the app or at disputes@tradezyhq.com explaining what was misrepresented.
3
Our team reviews the dispute and responds within 24 hours for HIGH severity cases.
🚫
Never feel pressured to complete a trade. Your safety and satisfaction come first.
Q
Can I add cash to a trade if our items aren't equal value?
▼
Yes — Tradezy fully supports hybrid trades where one party adds cash to balance the value difference. When making an offer you can include a cash amount alongside your item. Cash is exchanged in person at the meetup — Tradezy only collects its platform fee via card.
💡
Example: Your $80 item for their $100 item — you add $20 cash at the meetup to make it even. The platform fee is calculated on the total combined value.

Meetups & Exchanges
How in-person trades work on Tradezy
Q
How does the meetup work?
▼
Once an offer is accepted, both traders agree on a meetup time and location through Tradezy messages. After the in-person exchange happens, both parties confirm the meetup in the app. Here's the full flow:
1
Agree on location — choose a safe public spot via messages.
2
Meet up — inspect both items before exchanging.
3
Both confirm in the app — tap "Confirm Meetup" after the exchange.
4
Rate each other — ratings unlock after both confirmations. Builds your reputation.
Q
Where should I meet for a trade?
▼
Always meet in a public place with other people around. Great meetup spots include:
✓
Public parking lots — shopping centers, grocery stores
✓
Police station parking lots — many welcome safe exchange meetups
✓
Libraries, coffee shops, community centers
✗
Never meet at someone's home or in isolated areas
✗
Never share your home address in messages
Q
What does "verified meetup" mean?
▼
A verified meetup means both traders tapped confirm in the Tradezy app after the exchange. This creates a timestamped record that the trade happened. Verified meetups:

— Unlock the ratings system so both parties can leave reviews
— Create a permanent record for dispute purposes
— Count toward your trade history and trust score
— Are required before any ratings appear on your profile
✅
Only confirm the meetup after you have inspected the item and are satisfied. Once confirmed, the trade is considered complete.

Safety & Security
How Tradezy keeps you protected
Q
What prohibited items can't be traded on Tradezy?
▼
The following are strictly prohibited on Tradezy:

🚫 Firearms, weapons, ammunition, or explosives
🚫 Drugs, alcohol, or tobacco products
🚫 Live animals
🚫 Adult content or explicit material
🚫 Cryptocurrency or gift cards
🚫 Stolen or counterfeit goods
🚫 Any illegal items or services
⚠️
Listing prohibited items results in immediate removal and may result in a permanent ban. Every listing and message is screened automatically in real-time.
Q
What if someone tries to take the conversation off Tradezy?
▼
Keep all communication on Tradezy. Sharing phone numbers, email addresses, or social media handles in messages violates our Terms of Service and removes your trade protection. Tradezy's messaging system automatically detects and blocks attempts to move conversations off-platform.
🚨
If someone pressures you to communicate outside of Tradezy, report them immediately using the Report button. This is a common scam tactic.
Q
How do I report a problem with a user?
▼
Use the Report button on any listing, message, or user profile. You can also email disputes@tradezyhq.com directly. All reports are reviewed by our team within 24 hours. Reporting is anonymous — the other user will not know you reported them.
