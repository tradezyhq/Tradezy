Section 1
About Tradezy

Tradezy is an online peer-to-peer bartering marketplace that enables users to trade goods, food, water, and other items with other users in their local area. Tradezy is operated by Tradezy LLC, a Connecticut limited liability company.

Tradezy is a platform, not a party to any trade. We provide the technology, tools, escrow infrastructure, and dispute resolution services that enable trades to happen — but we are not the buyer, seller, or trader in any transaction. All trades are made directly between users.

Tradezy was designed to help communities during economically difficult times by enabling people to exchange goods locally without requiring cash, though cash add-ons are permitted at each trader's discretion.

Section 3
How Tradezy Works

Tradezy operates as a hyperlocal bartering marketplace. Here is how the platform works:

  1. List an item: Users post items they wish to trade. A minimum of 3 photos is required — front view, back view, and a detail or damage shot. Listing also requires an accurate condition rating (New / Like New / Good / Fair / Poor) and estimated value.
  2. Make or receive an offer: Users can offer to trade item-for-item, trade with a cash add-on, or make a cash-only offer depending on the seller's preferences. When making an offer, Trader B is required to upload a minimum of 3 photos of their item (front view, back view, and detail/damage) and select their item's condition rating. These are shown to the listing owner before they accept and are stored as dispute evidence.
  3. Agree to a trade: When both parties accept an offer, the 5% platform fee is immediately collected from both parties into Stripe escrow. The meetup cannot be scheduled until both payments have cleared.
  4. Schedule the meetup: Once both fees are confirmed in escrow, traders can schedule a meetup at a public location within their chosen radius. The meetup date and location are set through the platform.
  5. Meet locally and swap: Traders meet in person and complete the exchange.
  6. Confirm completion: Both parties confirm the trade is complete through the platform. Escrow is released to Tradezy as earned revenue.
  7. Rate your trader: Both parties leave ratings and reviews visible on the other's public profile.
📷
Photo & Condition Requirements: Both the listing owner (Trader A) and the person making an offer (Trader B) are required to upload a minimum of 3 photos of their respective items and select an accurate condition rating. These photos and ratings are stored automatically and used as evidence in any dispute about item condition. Submitting inaccurate photos or a false condition rating is a violation of these Terms.

Tradezy is not responsible for the quality, safety, legality, or accuracy of any items listed. Users trade at their own risk. Tradezy's trade protection program provides dispute resolution but does not guarantee the outcome of every dispute.

Section 6
Trade Protection & Escrow

When a trade offer is accepted, Tradezy initiates an escrow hold via Stripe on the 5% platform fee from both parties. This escrow serves the following purposes:

  • Protects both parties' fees until the trade is confirmed complete
  • Provides funds for refunds in the event of a dispute decided in a trader's favor
  • Allows Tradezy to freeze funds instantly when a dispute is filed

Payment is required before the meetup can be scheduled. Once both parties' 5% fees are confirmed in Stripe escrow, the platform unlocks the meetup scheduling feature. This ensures both parties have financial stake in completing the trade as agreed.

Trade Protection only applies to trades conducted entirely through the Tradezy platform. Trades arranged off-platform, including those where contact was made via phone, email, or social media before the trade was agreed, are not eligible for dispute resolution or escrow protection.

Escrow is held by Stripe and subject to Stripe's terms of service. Tradezy does not hold user funds directly.

Section 6b
Cancellation Policy

Because fees are collected at the time of offer acceptance, the following cancellation rules apply:

  • Offer declined (before escrow): No fee charged to either party. Full refund, instant.
  • Cancelled after escrow clears (before meetup): A cancellation fee of $0.50 or the Stripe processing cost (whichever is greater) is deducted from the cancelling party's refund. The other party receives a full refund of their escrow.
  • No-show at meetup: The party who did not show is subject to a dispute filed by the other party. If found at fault, their escrow fee is forfeited and applied to the other party's refund. Repeat no-shows result in account suspension.
  • Mutual cancellation: If both parties agree to cancel before the meetup, both receive full refunds minus the Stripe processing fee. Mutual cancellations do not count as strikes against either account.
  • Cancelled after meetup confirmed: Once both parties confirm the trade is complete, no refund is available. If there is a dispute about the item after confirmation, file a dispute within 14 days.
💡
Why we charge a cancellation fee: Stripe charges us a processing fee every time a card is charged and refunded. The cancellation fee covers this cost — we do not profit from cancellations. We want trading to be as low-friction as possible.

Tradezy provides a dispute resolution system for trades that go wrong. The following process applies:

  1. File a dispute within 14 days of trade completion through the Disputes section of your dashboard
  2. Escrow is immediately frozen for both parties upon dispute filing

  4. Tradezy's AI system (powered by Claude AI) reviews all evidence and suggests a resolution
  5. For low-to-medium severity cases, the AI resolution may be applied automatically
  6. High-severity cases, fraud allegations, and ban decisions always require human admin review
  7. A final decision is issued within 5 business days
  8. Decisions are binding — by using Tradezy you agree to accept dispute resolutions

Tradezy's dispute decisions are final. Filing a false dispute, submitting fabricated evidence, or misrepresenting facts in a dispute is a violation of these Terms and may result in immediate account suspension and forfeiture of your escrow funds.
